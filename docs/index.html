What lives in Oasis

Everything you reach for, in one quiet window.

One calm surface on localhost — no tabs to hunt through, no dashboard to log into. Just the tools you actually use, running on your machine.

The water

A living backdrop, not a wallpaper.

A looping cinemagraph of open sea sits behind everything. Four moods — dawn, day, dusk, night — follow your clock, or you pin one. Dark by design, so text always reads.

Ask

The hero search bar runs through your own local claude CLI. Ask a question, work a problem, or capture a thought — then turn any answer straight into a task, an idea, or a journal note. Nothing leaves your machine except the call you already make to Claude.

Ideas you can develop

Catch an idea in a line. Hit develop and Oasis opens it into four angles — the MVP cut, the wild version, the money angle, and the first build step — generated by your local Claude. Pin the keepers; turn any angle into a task.

Today

A drag-to-reorder task list that stays out of the way. Finished items quietly sink to the bottom.

Journal

A few lines and a mood, whenever you need to think out loud. A seven-day pulse shows how the week has felt.

Gallery

An auto-updating wall of the images you generate — it watches your Codex output folder — with a lightbox, copy-path, and reveal-in-folder. Paste a URL to keep any image.

Music

Score the work, your way.

Lo-Fi for heads-down beats. Ambient and downtempo for deep focus. Or Spotify — paste any link, or connect your own account and play your playlists.

Lo-Fi Ambient Spotify

Daily briefing

Once a day, one warm, grounding sentence about how your day is going — drawn from your tasks, journal and mood — with a quiet line of stats. A gentle check-in, not a productivity scoreboard.

Tool dock

Oasis auto-discovers the projects in your build folder — anything with a Launch *.bat or an npm run dev — and gives you one-click launch. No more digging through directories to start what you were working on.

Recent agent work

A quiet ticker of your latest Claude Code and Codex sessions. Click to see them all and pick up exactly where you left off.

Palette & focus timer

Hit Ctrl K to run anything from one box. Start a 25- or 50-minute focus session, set the scene, or jump to any tab.

Private and light

Zero npm dependencies, no account, no telemetry. Oasis runs on Node, opens in its own window, and keeps every byte of your data on your machine.

FAQ

Questions, answered.

Is it really free?
Yes. Oasis is free to download and use, with no account and no subscription. The only cost you ever pay is your own usage of Claude and Codex through the CLIs you already have.
What do I need to run it?
Windows 10 or 11 and a free copy of Node.js (the setup checks for it and links you if it's missing). Oasis has zero npm dependencies and opens in its own window, so it stays light even on modest hardware.
Do I need Claude Code and Codex?
They're recommended, not required. Ask and Ideas run through your local claude CLI, and the Gallery and ticker watch your Codex output. Without them, the water, Today, Journal, Music, Tool dock and the rest still work.
Is my data private?
Completely. Oasis runs as a tiny local app on localhost with no account and no telemetry. Your ideas, tasks, journal and images stay on your machine. The only thing that ever leaves is the call you already make to Claude through your own CLI.
How do I open it after setup?
Setup creates an Oasis desktop shortcut. Open it any time and Oasis starts its local server quietly and opens in its own window. You can also run Launch Oasis.bat from the folder.
